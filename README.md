# BME-450---project
## Title: Bird Identification Based On Sounds
## Team Members: Ainsley Gillcrist (agillcri), Akansha Chauhan (akanshachau)

### Description:  
# Bird watching is a common activity throughout Northern America. Many birdwatchers identify birds based on appearance; however, identification based on their songs is a neat way to identify birds that one may not be able to see. Each songbird species has its own song, and due to the prevalence of bird watching, there are multiple datasets of bird songs online. In order to narrow the scope of the project, we will focus on specific songbird species common to the state of Indiana. We will focus on warblers; specifically the Yellow Warbler, Magnolia Warbler, Yellow-rumped Warbler, and Black-and-White Warbler. The audio files we are using are from the Macaulay Library at Cornell University, home of Cornell Lab of Ornithology, with a world-renowned collection. Using a dataset from this source can help ensure the accuracy of the bird noises. Overall, the goal of this project is to create a neural network that can identify Indiana warblers based on their songs.
